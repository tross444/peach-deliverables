What the system does

Market Watch monitors 646 fintech lending companies daily for buying signals: new hires in lending roles, funding rounds, product launches, partnerships, and technology changes. When a signal fires, the system automatically finds the right decision-makers at that company, writes personalized outreach that references the specific signal, verifies every email address for deliverability, and loads everything into SmartLead ready for Hugo to review and send. No manual research, no copy-paste, no guesswork about timing.

What changes day-to-day

Hugo stops researching. Starts selling.

Right now, outreach starts with manual research. Who should we target? What's happening at their company? Who's the right person? What's the angle? That work takes hours per company, and the timing depends on luck.

With Market Watch, that front-end disappears. Hugo opens SmartLead and sees contacts who are there because something real happened at their company this week. The signal is in the email. The research is done. The copy is personalized to the specific event that triggered it.

Hugo's job shifts from finding opportunities to working them. He reviews the outreach, adds his relationship knowledge, decides when to press or hold back. His domain expertise gets amplified, not replaced.

Under the hood

The pipeline, end to end

Market Watch → 14 signals at 5 companies → Contact Forge → ICP filter → 10 verified contacts → Message Mill → 40 emails, 0 fabricated → SmartLead

Each step runs daily on a Linux server. No human intervention after initial setup. 180 automated tests cover the pipeline end-to-end.

Data integrity is the hard part, and we built for it. Every factual claim in every email traces back to source data. The PredictLeads signal that triggered outreach, the approved proof pool it references, or the enrichment record it cites. Today's test: 19 claims traced, zero fabricated. The generation system doesn't invent facts. It references them.

There's also a freshness filter. Signal APIs sometimes index old events with new dates. The system parses actual event dates from signal text and drops anything older than 90 days. Today's test caught and removed one stale signal before any email was written.

Monthly run cost: ~$239 in API fees (signal detection, enrichment, AI generation, email verification). No manual research time.
